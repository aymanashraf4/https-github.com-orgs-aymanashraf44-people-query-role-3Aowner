Vitamins for building muscle There are several important vitamins to build muscle in the body, which is as follows: Vitamin D: is one of the important vitamins to increase the immunity of the body, in addition to its role in the absorption of phosphorus, calcium important in building bones, and in metabolism. Vitamin C is one of the most important vitamins for the skin. It contributes to the formation of collagen and elastin, which are responsible for the freshness of the skin. It also contributes to the supply of nutrients and oxygen to the muscles. It can be obtained from strawberries, tomatoes, cauliflower and others. Vitamin E: One of the types of antioxidants, it is obtained from sunflower oil, corn oil, avocado, spinach, and carrots. B vitamins are a vital component of healthy muscle building. Vitamin B1 (Thiamine) is an important vitamin for protein metabolism, as well as hemoglobin, obtained from rice, meat, and nuts. Vitamin B2 (riboflavin): contributes to metabolism, oxidation of fatty acids, glucose metabolism, and is obtained from milk, eggs. Vitamin B3 (niacin): Its importance is in the production of energy, and can be obtained from fish, legumes, milk, and eggs. Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine): Contributes to the growth and metabolism of proteins, and can be obtained from brown rice, fish, and soybeans. Vitamin B12 (Cyanocopalamin): Important in the metabolism of fats and carbohydrates, in addition to building nerve tissue, can be obtained from poultry, liver, and meat methods. Vitamin B7 (Biotin): Important in the metabolism of important amino acids in building protein, it can be obtained from yeast, egg yolk
